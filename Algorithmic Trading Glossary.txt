// Glossary of trading terminology for algorithmic traders
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
100% Profitable Martingale Strategy = A trading strategy that involves doubling the position size after each loss, with the aim of recovering all previous losses and making a profit when a winning trade eventually occurs. Note: This strategy carries significant risk and can lead to substantial losses.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1987 crash = A significant stock market crash that occurred on October 19, 1987, also known as "Black Monday," when stock markets around the world experienced a sudden and severe decline in value.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Active manager = An investment manager who actively makes decisions about buying and selling securities in an attempt to outperform a specific benchmark or index.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Active strategy = A trading approach that involves actively making decisions about buying and selling securities based on market analysis, trends, and other factors, with the goal of generating higher returns than a passive strategy.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADX = See: Average directional index
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AI agent = An artificial intelligence system that can perceive its environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve specific goals, often used in algorithmic trading to automate decision-making processes.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AIC = See: Akaike Information Criterion
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Akaike Information Criterion = A statistical measure used to compare the goodness of fit of different models while penalizing for model complexity, helping to select the best model among a set of candidates.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithmic tacit collusion = A situation where competing algorithmic trading systems unintentionally coordinate their actions to maintain higher prices or reduce competition, without explicit communication between human traders.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithmic trader = A trader who uses computer algorithms to automate the process of executing trades based on predefined criteria and strategies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithmic trading = The use of computer algorithms to automatically execute trades based on predefined criteria and strategies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alpha = A measure of an investment's performance relative to a benchmark index, representing the excess return generated by the investment.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alpha generation platform = A software system or framework designed to facilitate the development, testing, and deployment of trading strategies aimed at generating alpha.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alpha-seeking market timing = A trading strategy that aims to generate alpha by actively timing market entry and exit points based on various indicators and analysis.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alternative data = Non-traditional data sources used in financial analysis and trading, such as social media sentiment, satellite imagery, or web traffic, to gain insights beyond conventional market data.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alternative investment = An investment in assets other than traditional stocks, bonds, or cash, such as real estate, private equity, hedge funds, or commodities.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alternative trading system = A non-exchange trading venue that matches buyers and sellers of securities, often providing greater flexibility and lower costs compared to traditional exchanges.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Analyst = A professional who evaluates financial data, market trends, and economic conditions to provide investment recommendations and insights.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Animal spirits" = A term coined by economist John Maynard Keynes to describe the emotional and psychological factors that influence investor behavior and decision-making in financial markets.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Annualized return = The geometric average amount of money earned by an investment each year over a given time period.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Arbitrage = The simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset in different markets to profit from price discrepancies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Arbitrage pricing theory = A multi-factor financial model that explains the expected return of an asset based on various macroeconomic factors and their associated risk premiums.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
APT = See: Arbitrage pricing theory
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ARCH = Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity, a statistical model used to analyze and forecast time series data with changing volatility over time.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ARIMA = AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average, a class of statistical models used for analyzing and forecasting time series data.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ARMA = AutoRegressive Moving Average, a class of statistical models used for analyzing and forecasting time series data by combining autoregressive and moving average components.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ask = The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security or asset.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ask/Bid spread = The difference between the ask price and the bid price of a security or asset, representing the transaction cost for traders.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Asset = Anything of value that is owned by an individual or organization, including stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Asset management = The professional management of various securities and assets to meet specified investment goals for the benefit of investors.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Asymmetric information = A situation in which one party in a transaction has more or better information than the other party, often leading to market inefficiencies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Auction-based trading = A trading mechanism where buyers and sellers submit bids and offers during specific time intervals, and trades are executed at a single clearing price determined by the highest bid and lowest ask.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Auction theory = A branch of economics that studies how auction designs and rules affect the behavior of bidders and the outcomes of auctions.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AUM = Assets Under Management, the total market value of assets that an investment company or financial institution manages on behalf of clients.

Autism = A developmental disorder characterized by difficulties with social interaction, communication, and repetitive behaviors. Autistic persons are sometimes associated with uncanny and very narrow mathematical skills, but this is very rare. Microsoft has often targeted and recruited autistic people for low-level programming jobs, but the development of AI has made many of these task-based jobs obsolete.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Autocorrelation = A statistical measure that indicates the degree of similarity between a given time series and a lagged version of itself over successive time intervals.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Autoregression = A type of statistical model used for analyzing time series data, where current values are regressed on their own previous values.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Average directional index (ADX) = A technical indicator used to quantify the strength of a trend in a financial market.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Average true range (ATR) = A technical analysis indicator that measures market volatility by decomposing the entire range of an asset price for that period.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Backtesting = The process of testing a trading strategy or model using historical data to evaluate its performance before applying it in real-time trading.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Baron Munchhausen = A fictional character who lifted himself out of a swamp by his own hair, or in other words pulled himself up by his own bootsraps. See: Bootstrapping.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Base rate = The benchmark interest rate set by a central bank, which influences borrowing costs and overall economic activity.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Basel I = The first set of international banking regulations established by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision in 1988, aimed at ensuring that financial institutions maintain adequate capital to cover their risks.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Basel II = The second set of international banking regulations established by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision in 2004, which introduced more comprehensive risk management practices and capital requirements for banks.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Basel III = The third set of international banking regulations established by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision in 2010, aimed at strengthening bank capital requirements and introducing new regulatory requirements on bank liquidity and leverage.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Basis point = The minimum price movement of a trading instrument, often used to measure the change in value between two prices.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BATS = Better Alternative Trading System, an electronic stock exchange that provides trading services to institutional investors and was acquired by Cboe Global Markets in 2017.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bayesian optimization = A statistical technique used to optimize complex functions by building a probabilistic model of the objective function and using it to select the most promising parameters to evaluate.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bearish = A market sentiment characterized by falling prices and pessimism about future market performance.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bear market = A market condition characterized by a prolonged period of declining prices, often defined as a drop of 20% or more from recent highs.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Beat the house = To achieve investment returns that consistently outperform the average market returns, similar to how a gambler aims to win against the odds set by a casino.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Beat the market = To achieve investment returns that exceed the performance of a specific benchmark or index.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Behavioral finance = A field of study that combines psychology and economics to understand how cognitive biases and emotional factors influence financial decision-making and market behavior.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Benchmark = A standard or point of reference against which the performance of a security, portfolio, or investment manager can be measured.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Benchmarking = The process of comparing the performance of a trading strategy or investment against a benchmark to evaluate its effectiveness.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Best execution = The obligation of brokers and investment firms to execute client orders in a manner that provides the most favorable terms for the client, considering factors such as price, speed, and likelihood of execution.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Beta = A measure of a security's sensitivity to market movements, indicating its volatility relative to the overall market.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bias = A systematic error or deviation from the true value in a model or trading strategy, often resulting from incorrect assumptions or data.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bid = The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security or asset.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Black box = A system or model whose internal workings are not visible or understood by the user, often used in the context of trading algorithms where the decision-making process is opaque.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Black Monday" = The stock market crash that occurred on October 19, 1987, when stock markets around the world experienced a sudden and severe decline in value.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Black-Scholes equation = A mathematical model used for pricing European-style options, which calculates the theoretical value of options based on factors such as the underlying asset price, strike price, time to expiration, risk-free interest rate, and volatility.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Black Swan = An unpredictable and rare event that has significant consequences, often used in finance to describe market events that are beyond normal expectations and have a major impact on markets.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Blockchain = A decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across multiple computers in a way that ensures security, transparency, and immutability, often used in cryptocurrencies and other financial applications.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bloomberg = A global financial services company that provides real-time data, news, analytics, and trading platforms to financial professionals.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bollinger bands strategy = A trading strategy that uses Bollinger Bands, which are volatility bands placed above and below a moving average, to identify potential buy and sell signals.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bond = A fixed-income security that represents a loan made by an investor to a borrower, typically corporate or governmental, which pays periodic interest and returns the principal at maturity.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Book value = The net asset value of a company, calculated as total assets minus total liabilities, representing the value of the company if it were to be liquidated.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bootstrapping = A statistical method used to estimate the distribution of a sample statistic by resampling with replacement from the original data, often used in finance to assess the uncertainty of model parameters.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Brady report = A report published in 1988 by the Brady Commission, which investigated the causes of the 1987 stock market crash and provided recommendations to improve market stability and investor confidence.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Broker = A financial intermediary that facilitates the buying and selling of securities on behalf of clients. Hopefully not someone who makes you broke
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Broker fees = The costs associated with executing trades through a brokerage, which can include commissions, spreads, and other transaction fees.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Broker routing = The process by which a broker directs client orders to specific exchanges or market makers for execution, often based on factors such as price, speed, and liquidity.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Brownian motion = A mathematical model used to describe the random movement of particles, often applied in finance to model the unpredictable behavior of asset prices over time.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bubble = A market condition characterized by rapid and unsustainable price increases, often driven by speculative behavior, followed by a sharp decline or crash.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bullion = Physical precious metals, such as gold or silver, typically in the form of bars or coins, used for investment or as a store of value.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bullish = A market sentiment characterized by rising prices and optimism about future market performance.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bull market = A market condition characterized by a prolonged period of rising prices, often driven by strong investor confidence and economic growth.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Bull run = A sustained period of rising prices in the stock market or a specific asset class, often driven by positive investor sentiment and economic growth.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Buy-and-Hold strategy = A passive investment strategy that involves buying a security and holding it for a long period, regardless of market fluctuations.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Buy on weakness = A trading strategy that involves purchasing a security when its price declines, with the expectation that it will rebound in value.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) = A measure of an investment's annual growth rate over time, taking into account the effect of compounding.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Calendar day = A standard day in the calendar, including weekends and holidays, as opposed to a trading day which only includes days when markets are open.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Calmar ratio = A risk-adjusted performance metric that compares the annualized return of an investment to its maximum drawdown.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cancel = The action of withdrawing or nullifying a previously placed order in the market before it is executed.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Capex = Capital expenditures, which are funds used by a company to acquire, upgrade, and maintain physical assets such as property, buildings, or equipment.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Capital = Financial assets or resources available for investment or trading.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Capital asset pricing model (CAPM) = A financial model that describes the relationship between systematic risk and expected return for assets, particularly stocks.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Capitalism = An economic system characterized by private ownership of the means of production and the creation of goods and services for profit in a competitive market.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CAPM = See: Capital asset pricing model
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cap-weighted market index = A stock market index in which each constituent stock is weighted according to its total market capitalization, giving more influence to larger companies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GARCH = Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity, a statistical model used to estimate and forecast the volatility of financial time series data.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Catastrophe theory = A branch of mathematics that studies how small changes in circumstances can lead to sudden and dramatic shifts in behavior, often applied in financial markets to analyze abrupt market movements.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cauchy distribution = A probability distribution characterized by heavy tails and a peak at its median, often used in financial modeling to represent asset returns with extreme variations.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Causality = The relationship between cause and effect, often analyzed in financial markets to determine how one variable influences another.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CFTC = Commodity Futures Trading Commission, a U.S. government agency responsible for regulating the futures and options markets to protect market participants and ensure market integrity.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Chaos theory = A branch of mathematics that studies complex systems and their unpredictable behavior, often applied in financial markets to analyze nonlinear dynamics and market volatility.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Classification = A supervised machine learning task that involves predicting the category or class of a given input based on its features.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Closed position = A trading position that has been exited or liquidated, meaning the trader no longer holds any exposure to the underlying asset.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Closing a position = The act of exiting a trading position by selling an asset that was previously bought (for long positions) or buying back an asset that was previously sold (for short positions).
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Closing price = The final price at which a security is traded on a given trading day.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Clustering algorithms = Statistical methods used to group similar data points together based on their characteristics, often used in market segmentation and pattern recognition.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cointegrated = A statistical property of two or more time series variables that indicates a long-term equilibrium relationship, meaning that they move together over time despite short-term fluctuations.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Collateralised debt obligations (CDOs) = Financial instruments that pool together various types of debt, such as mortgages or loans, and divide them into tranches with different risk levels and returns for investors.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Commodity = A basic good used in commerce that is interchangeable with other goods of the same type, such as gold, oil, or agricultural products.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Commodity Exchange = A marketplace where commodities are traded, such as the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) or the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX).
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Comnsumer price index = A measure that examines the weighted average of prices of a basket of consumer goods and services, used to assess inflation and cost of living.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Confidence interval = A range of values derived from sample data that is likely to contain the true population parameter with a specified level of confidence.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Continuous trading = A trading mechanism that allows for the buying and selling of securities throughout the trading day, as opposed to auction-based trading that occurs at specific times.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Convex optimization = A subfield of optimization that deals with problems where the objective function is convex, meaning that any local minimum is also a global minimum, often used in portfolio optimization and risk management.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Copy trading = A trading strategy that involves replicating the trades of experienced and successful traders, allowing less experienced investors to benefit from their expertise.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cost = The total expenses incurred in executing trades, including broker fees, spreads, and other transaction costs.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Correlation = A statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two or more variables move in relation to each other.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Correlation coefficient = A numerical measure that quantifies the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables, typically ranging from -1 to +1.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Correlation of defaults = A measure of the likelihood that multiple borrowers will default on their loans simultaneously, often used in credit risk modeling.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Counter-intuitive distributions = Statistical distributions that exhibit unexpected or non-standard properties, often challenging traditional assumptions in financial modeling and risk assessment.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Covariance = A statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two variables change together, used to assess the relationship between asset returns in portfolio management.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Covariance matrix = A square matrix that contains the covariances between pairs of variables, often used in finance to analyze the relationships between multiple asset returns.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CPI = See: Consumer Price Index
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Credit = The ability to borrow money or access goods and services with the promise to pay later, often used in financial markets to facilitate trading and investment.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Credit rating agency = An organization that evaluates the creditworthiness of issuers of debt securities and assigns ratings based on their assessment of the issuer's ability to meet its financial obligations. E.g. Standard & Poor's, Moody's, Fitch Ratings.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Credit swap = A financial derivative contract in which one party agrees to compensate another party in the event of a credit event, such as a default, on a specified debt instrument.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cross-validation = A statistical method used to assess the performance of a model by partitioning the data into subsets, training the model on one subset, and validating it on another.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Crypto = See: Cryptocurrency
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cryptocurrency = A digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates independently of a central authority, such as Bitcoin or Ethereum.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Currency = A system of money in common use, particularly in a nation, used as a medium of exchange for goods and services.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cycle = A recurring pattern or trend in market data that repeats over a specific period, often analyzed to identify potential trading opportunities.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cycle decomposition = The process of breaking down a time series into its constituent cycles to analyze and understand the underlying patterns and trends in the data.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dark pool = A private financial forum or exchange for trading securities that is not accessible to the general public, allowing institutional investors to trade large blocks of shares without revealing their intentions to the broader market.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data broker = An entity that collects, aggregates, and sells data, often including personal or financial information, to other businesses or individuals for various purposes, including marketing and analytics.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data-snooping bias = A statistical bias that occurs when a model is excessively fitted to historical data, leading to over-optimistic performance estimates that may not hold in out-of-sample testing.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Debt = An amount of money owed by one party to another, often incurred through borrowing or credit.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Decimalization = The process of converting the pricing of securities from fractions to decimal format, which improves price transparency and market efficiency.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Deep learning = A subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with multiple layers to model complex patterns in data, often used in financial applications for predictive analytics and algorithmic trading.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Debit = An entry recording an amount owed, representing a decrease in assets or an increase in liabilities in accounting.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DeFi = Decentralized Finance, a financial system built on blockchain technology that operates without traditional intermediaries like banks, allowing for peer-to-peer transactions and services.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Defined role = A specific function or responsibility assigned to an individual or system within a trading operation, such as risk management, trade execution, or strategy development.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Delta = A measure of the sensitivity of an option's price to changes in the price of the underlying asset.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Delta neutral = An options trading strategy that aims to offset the delta of a portfolio, making it insensitive to small price movements in the underlying asset.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Demand = The total amount of a specific good or service that consumers are willing and able to purchase at various prices in the market.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Deregulation = The process of removing or reducing government regulations and restrictions in a particular industry, often aimed at promoting competition and efficiency.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Derivatives = Financial instruments whose value is derived from the value of an underlying asset, such as stocks, bonds, commodities, or market indices.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Detrending = The process of removing trends from time series data to focus on the underlying patterns or cycles, often used in financial analysis to better understand market behavior.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dimensionality reduction = A set of techniques used to reduce the number of features or variables in a dataset while retaining important information, often used in machine learning to improve model performance and reduce overfitting.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Diminishing edge = The phenomenon where the effectiveness or profitability of a trading strategy decreases over time as more market participants adopt similar strategies, leading to reduced opportunities for excess returns.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Direct Edge = A former electronic stock exchange that provided trading services to institutional investors and was acquired by BATS Global Markets in 2014.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Directional change algorithms = Statistical methods used to identify significant price movements in financial markets by filtering out minor fluctuations, helping traders focus on meaningful trends.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Directional trader = A trader who takes positions based on the anticipated direction of market movements, either bullish or bearish.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Direct market access (DMA) = A trading mechanism that allows traders to place orders directly into the market without the need for a broker's intervention, often used by institutional investors and high-frequency traders.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Discounted price = The present value of a future cash flow or payment, calculated by applying a discount rate to account for the time value of money.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Distribution = A statistical function that describes the likelihood of different outcomes in a random variable.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DJI = See: Dow Jones Industrial Average
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dollar Average Strategy (DAS) = A trading strategy that involves investing a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the asset's price, to reduce the impact of volatility and lower the average cost per share over time.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dotcom bubble = A period of excessive speculation in internet-related companies during the late 1990s, leading to a market bubble that eventually burst in the early 2000s, resulting in significant losses for investors.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Double EMA indicator = An indicator that uses two Exponential Moving Averages (EMAs) to smooth price data and identify trends more effectively than a single EMA.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dow Jones Industrial Average = A stock market index that measures the stock performance of 30 large, publicly-owned companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Dow Theory" = A financial theory that suggests that the stock market moves in predictable trends and that these trends can be identified through the analysis of market indices, particularly the Dow Jones Industrial Average and the Dow Jones Transportation Average.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- 
Drawdown = A measure of the decline in the value of an investment or trading strategy from its peak to its trough, often expressed as a percentage.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Drift = A consistent, long-term trend in the price movement of a financial asset, often influenced by factors such as economic growth, inflation, and market sentiment.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DSP = Digital Signal Processing, a field of study that focuses on the analysis and manipulation of signals, often used in financial markets to process and analyze time series data for trading strategies.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dual moving average crossover = A trading strategy that uses two moving averages of different lengths to generate buy and sell signals based on their crossover points.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Durbin-Watson = A statistical test used to detect the presence of autocorrelation in the residuals of a regression analysis.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, a financial metric used to assess a company's operating performance by excluding non-operational expenses.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ECM = See: Error Correction Models
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Economic growth (g) = The increase in the inflation-adjusted market value of the goods and services produced by an economy over time, often measured as the percentage increase in real gross domestic product (GDP).
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Edge = A competitive advantage or unique characteristic of a trading strategy that allows it to generate consistent profits over time. You don't have to know Bono to find the edge.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Edge ratio = A performance metric that compares the average profit of winning trades to the average loss of losing trades in a trading strategy.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) = A financial theory that suggests that asset prices fully reflect all available information, making it impossible to consistently achieve higher returns than the overall market through stock selection or market timing.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Electronic exchange = A digital platform that facilitates the trading of financial instruments electronically, allowing buyers and sellers to transact without the need for a physical trading floor.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Electronic trading = The use of computer systems and networks to facilitate the buying and selling of financial instruments electronically, often through online trading platforms.

EMH = See: Efficient Market Hypothesis

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Event probability = The likelihood of a specific event occurring, often used in risk management and trading strategies to assess potential outcomes.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Genetic algorithm = A search heuristic that mimics the process of natural selection to find optimal or near-optimal solutions to complex problems, often used in trading strategy optimization.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Exchange = A marketplace where securities, commodities, derivatives, and other financial instruments are bought and sold.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Exit point = The specific price level or condition at which a trader decides to close a position in a trading strategy.
